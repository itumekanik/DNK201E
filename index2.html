📖 Curvilinear Motion in Polar Coordinates

🔹 Problem Statement

A slotted link is pinned at O and rotates with constant angular velocity θ̇. It drives a peg P along a spiral guide defined by r = 0.4θ (m), where θ is in radians. Determine the radial and transverse components of velocity and acceleration of P at the instant θ = π/3 rad.

🔹 Given & Find

Given

\( \dot{\theta} = \text{const} \) (angular velocity)

\( \ddot{\theta} = 0 \) (constant ω)

\( r = 0.4\theta \) (spiral equation)

Find at \( \theta = \pi/3 \)

\( v_r, \; v_\theta \) (velocity components)

\( a_r, \; a_\theta \) (acceleration components)

🔹 Polar Coordinate Formulas

In polar coordinates, the velocity and acceleration components are:

$$ v_r = \dot{r} \qquad v_\theta = r\dot{\theta} $$
$$ a_r = \ddot{r} - r\dot{\theta}^2 \qquad a_\theta = r\ddot{\theta} + 2\dot{r}\dot{\theta} $$

🔹 Time Derivatives of r

Since \( r = 0.4\theta \), differentiating with respect to time:

$$ \dot{r} = 0.4\dot{\theta} $$
$$ \ddot{r} = 0.4\ddot{\theta} = 0 \quad (\text{since } \dot{\theta} = \text{const}) $$

🔹 Velocity Components

$$ v_r = \dot{r} = 0.4\dot{\theta} $$
$$ v_\theta = r\dot{\theta} = 0.4\theta\dot{\theta} $$

🔹 Acceleration Components

$$ a_r = \ddot{r} - r\dot{\theta}^2 = 0 - 0.4\theta\dot{\theta}^2 = -0.4\theta\dot{\theta}^2 $$
$$ a_\theta = r\ddot{\theta} + 2\dot{r}\dot{\theta} = 0 + 2(0.4\dot{\theta})\dot{\theta} = 0.8\dot{\theta}^2 $$
