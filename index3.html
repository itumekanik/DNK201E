📖 Theoretical Solution — Circular Satellite Orbit

🔹 Problem Statement

A satellite travels indefinitely in a circular orbit if its normal acceleration equals the gravitational acceleration at that altitude:

$$ a_n = g\left(\frac{R}{r}\right)^2 $$

where g = surface gravity, R = Earth's radius, r = R + h = distance from Earth's center.

🔹 Core Derivation

Normal (centripetal) acceleration for circular motion:

$$ a_n = \frac{v^2}{r} $$

Setting equal to gravitational field at altitude r:

$$ \frac{v^2}{r} = \frac{gR^2}{r^2} \quad \Rightarrow \quad v = \sqrt{\frac{gR^2}{r}} \quad \text{or} \quad r = \frac{gR^2}{v^2} $$
$$ h = r - R = \frac{gR^2}{v^2} - R $$

🔹 Unit Systems — Active: SI
