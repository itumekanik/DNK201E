📖 Theoretical Solution — Circular Satellite Orbit

🔹 Problem Statement

A satellite travels in a circular orbit around the Earth. For indefinite circular orbit, the normal component of the satellite's acceleration must equal the gravitational acceleration at that altitude:

$$ a_n = g\left(\frac{R}{r}\right)^2 $$

where g = surface gravity, R = Earth's radius, r = distance from Earth's center to satellite.

Three problems all use this condition — differing only in what is known/unknown and unit system.

🔹 Core Derivation

For circular motion, the normal (centripetal) acceleration is:

$$ a_n = \frac{v^2}{r} $$

Setting this equal to the gravitational acceleration at radius r:

$$ \frac{v^2}{r} = g\left(\frac{R}{r}\right)^2 = \frac{gR^2}{r^2} $$

Solving for orbital speed:

$$ v = \sqrt{\frac{g R^2}{r}} \qquad \text{or} \qquad r = \frac{gR^2}{v^2} $$

And since r = R + h, the altitude is:

$$ h = r - R = \frac{gR^2}{v^2} - R $$
