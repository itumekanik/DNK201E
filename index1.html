📖 Theoretical Solution — Vertical Projectile Motion

🔹 Problem Statement

A ball is tossed with a velocity of v₀ directed vertically upward from a window located y₀ above the ground. Knowing that the acceleration of the ball is constant and equal to g downward, determine:

  1. The velocity v and elevation y of the ball above the ground at any time t
  2. The highest elevation reached by the ball and the corresponding value of t
  3. The time when the ball will hit the ground and the corresponding velocity

Draw the v–t and y–t curves.

🔹 Given & Find

Given

\( v_0 \) = initial velocity (upward)

\( y_0 \) = window height above ground

\( a = -g \) = constant acceleration (downward)

Find

(a) \( v(t) \) and \( y(t) \)

(b) \( y_{\max} \) and corresponding \( t \)

(c) \( t \) when \( y = 0 \) and corresponding \( v \)

🔹 Part (a): General Equations of Motion

Since the acceleration is constant, we use the uniformly accelerated motion equations. Taking the positive direction as upward and origin at ground level:

$$ v(t) = v_0 + a \cdot t = v_0 - g \cdot t $$
$$ y(t) = y_0 + v_0 \cdot t + \frac{1}{2} a \cdot t^2 = y_0 + v_0 t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2 $$

These equations describe the velocity and position of the ball at any time \( t \geq 0 \).

🔹 Part (b): Maximum Elevation

At the highest point, the velocity is zero. Setting \( v(t) = 0 \):

$$ 0 = v_0 - g \cdot t_{\text{top}} \quad \Rightarrow \quad t_{\text{top}} = \frac{v_0}{g} $$

Substituting \( t_{\text{top}} \) into the position equation:

$$ y_{\max} = y_0 + v_0 \cdot \frac{v_0}{g} - \frac{1}{2} g \left( \frac{v_0}{g} \right)^2 = y_0 + \frac{v_0^2}{2g} $$

🔹 Part (c): Time to Hit Ground

When the ball hits the ground, \( y = 0 \). Setting \( y(t) = 0 \):

$$ 0 = y_0 + v_0 t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2 $$

Rearranging into standard quadratic form \( at^2 + bt + c = 0 \):

$$ \frac{1}{2} g \cdot t^2 - v_0 \cdot t - y_0 = 0 $$

Applying the quadratic formula and taking the positive root:

$$ t_{\text{ground}} = \frac{v_0 + \sqrt{v_0^2 + 2 g y_0}}{g} $$

The velocity at impact:

$$ v_{\text{ground}} = v_0 - g \cdot t_{\text{ground}} $$

🔹 Graphs

The v–t curve is a straight line with slope \( -g \) and y-intercept \( v_0 \).

The y–t curve is a downward-opening parabola starting at \( y_0 \), peaking at \( y_{\max} \), and returning to zero at \( t_{\text{ground}} \).
